# Overclaim Guardrails

Do not claim:

1. Real QDM/NV validation is complete.
2. Real CAD/Gerber/GDS validation is complete.
3. PyPEEC is ground-truth real physics.
4. U-Net or topology-aware U-Net is a final reliable via detector.
5. No-via false positive is solved under real distributions.
6. Return-path diagnosis is solved under real distributions.
7. Few-shot generated-domain adaptation proves real-world adaptation.
8. Primary-label correctness means mechanism-level explanation.
9. DivB sanity alone solves inverse ambiguity.
10. PDN/KCL generation is automatically realistic without external validation.
11. A high accepted accuracy at low coverage is a full-coverage detector claim.
12. A hidden rejection rate is sufficient without accepted-hidden risk.
13. A lowest-residual model is automatically the correct physical hypothesis.
14. Synthetic multi-state improvement proves hardware-feasible active
    measurement.
15. Real-data interface scaffolding means real-data validation has been done.
16. The generated PDN/KCL prototype proves real-board PDN robustness.
17. Matched generated H0/H1/H2/H3 hypothesis scoring proves mechanism-level
    correctness under real layouts.
18. The generated chip-like E11 PDN family proves real chip PDN realism.
19. E12 label accuracy plus KCL/current-closure consistency proves
    mechanism-level explanation.
20. Physics-aware generated graph projection proves real learned physics under
    imported CAD/GDS or real QDM/NV measurements.
21. A CI smoke/test pass is a fresh full-run experiment.
22. Metrics metadata normalization is new scientific evidence; it is only an
    audit-contract repair after evidence commands rewrite outputs.
23. The generated-domain differentiable forward layer validates against real
    devices, real CAD/GDS layouts, or external solvers.
24. The differentiable forward being a linear FFT operator guarantees correct
    gradient-based optimization under real measurement noise.
25. The physics-constrained differentiable inverse (E18) universally outperforms
    all baselines; dense-via recall is 0 and deep-layer misallocation >0.3.
26. KCL residual improvement from constrained optimization proves current
    accuracy improvement; KCL-RMSE tradeoff is documented.
27. A generated-domain unified leaderboard proves real-hardware ranking.
28. Warm-started L-BFGS-B with post-projection is optimal; hyperparameters
    are tuned on the generated distribution only.
29. A first-principles strategy taxonomy is experimental evidence. It is only
    a design input and cannot support or upgrade claims by itself.
30. Exhaustive direction coverage at the operator/decision/validation level
    proves practical recovery of all chip currents. It only bounds where
    breakthroughs can enter; each direction still needs evidence.
31. A Claude Code worker result is evidence before Codex audit and research
    graph registration. Worker output is implementation material until tested,
    audited, and linked to claims.
32. A generated-domain positive raw distance or profile residual gap is a major
    current-inversion breakthrough unless it survives noise, calibration, and
    operator-radius subtraction as a positive robust margin.
33. Network-solved topology profile inversion proves chip reverse analysis
    before real CAD/GDS-derived graphs, external forward validation, and real
    QDM/NV sanity gates exist.
34. E28 Gram transfer-matrix quotient separability proves full H0/H1/H2/H3
    recovery. E28 currently supports only Q0={H0}, Q12={H1,H2}, Q3={H3};
    H1_via versus H2_model_gap remains unresolved.
35. E28 generated-domain gain-nuisance survival proves real chip reverse
    analysis. The result still needs broader layout ensembles, external solver
    held-out transfer matrices, and real-calibrated QDM/NV nuisance.
36. E28 H1/H2 phase-diagram rows prove H1_via versus H2_model_gap is impossible
    in all settings. The audit is generated-domain and scoped to the current
    H2 via-factor/sheet-jitter axes, observation protocol, Gram representation,
    and nuisance budget.
37. Directional statistical Gamma proves real detection risk or full
    hypothesis recovery. It is a generated-domain matched-filter audit under
    explicit Gaussian/noise-direction assumptions and still needs external
    solver and real-calibrated nuisance validation.
38. E30 dual Schur local-access positive Gamma proves pad-feasible active
    probing or real chip reverse analysis. E30 is generated-domain, central
    via-open only, and uses candidate endpoint excitation as an observability
    upper bound; it still needs boundary-port feasibility, broader layouts,
    external solver rho, CAD/GDS graphs, and real QDM/NV sanity gates.
39. E31 pad-Schur positive Gamma proves real pad feasibility or chip reverse
    analysis. E31 is generated-domain and assumes candidate-projection surface
    pads plus a reference pad are available; it does not validate pad pitch,
    contact resistance, package parasitics, finite-width/external-solver gaps,
    CAD/GDS-derived graphs, or real QDM/NV measurements.
40. E32 pad-pitch reachability floors prove real design-for-test feasibility or
    real chip reverse analysis. E32 is generated-domain reachability evidence
    only; it does not add a fresh magnetic Gamma certificate beyond E31 and does
    not validate contact resistance, package parasitics, current/voltage driver
    limits, CAD/GDS-derived graphs, external solvers, or real QDM/NV
    measurements.
41. E33 certified observable current-subspace inversion proves full current
    density recovery or real chip reverse analysis. E33 recovers only the
    Fisher-stable current projection in a generated Fourier/thin-sheet model and
    explicitly refuses dark modes; it does not validate finite-width conductors,
    graph/Hodge/CAD-like bases, external solvers, or real QDM/NV measurements.
